Our Parish History

Sacred Heart Parish was formally established by Archbishop Joseph Hurley on June 26, 1959. Msgr. Leo Danaher was the founding pastor. Msgr. Patrick Madden became the new pastor of Sacred Heart in 1970. He ministered to the parish until his retirement in June of 2001.

Much of the building and development of the parish happened during this period. Rev. Anthony Sebra was appointed pastor in June of 2001. Rev. Victor Narivelil, CMI was appointed pastor in February 2007. He served as pastor until his retirement in August of 2014. Rev. James Kaniparampil, CMI. was appointed Pastor of Sacred Heart in August 2014 and continues to lead our parish today. The parish built a rectory in 1953 and in 1983 a second floor was added with an extensive remodel of the entire building in 2002.

The first building to be erected on Sacred Heart’s property was the original church, dedicated in May of 1960. It was remodeled after the present church was dedicated in September 1998 and is currently our Family Life Center. Our present church sanctuary was dedicated in 1998.

Our school began in 1960 with portable buildings situated where the current gymnasium now stands. The parish built a convent in 1962 to house the Sisters of Mercy from Clonakilty, Ireland. The Sisters of Mercy educated our children and provided spiritual guidance for our parish for more than 50 years.

The gymnasium was dedicated in 1998; the Pre-K (4-year-old) building was dedicated in 2005; and the Parish Center was dedicated in November 2012.

In 2017, the convent was renovated to house the Mercedarian Sisters of the Blessed Sacrament who arrived at Sacred Heart in October 2017.

A Message From Father James

Welcome!

Whether you are a visitor, new to the area, interested in becoming Catholic, seeking to return to the practice of your faith, or an active member of our parish, I along with the parishioners and school families of Sacred Heart Catholic Church and School welcome you to our spiritual home. We are pleased that you have come to our website and we want to do all that we can to assist you.

Sacred Heart Catholic Church, located on the west-side of Jacksonville, is a family-like community with rich cultural diversity while remaining one in faith and liturgy. We try to put our love into actions by works of charity through outreach programs. We provide ample opportunities to grow in faith through ministries like Religious Education for children, Youth Ministry, and Adult Faith Formation. We share in the upkeep of the parish by inviting members to participate in stewardship, pastoral, and social ministries. Our Parish School, staffed by the Mercedarian Sisters of the Blessed Sacrament and many dynamic teachers, provides excellent human and spiritual formation for our young people.

If you are new to the area or looking for a church, I want to invite you to come visit and celebrate the Eucharist with us. The parish staff and I will be glad to be of help to you in any way possible. If you have any questions, please do not hesitate to call us or email me or any of the staff personnel. Please refer to our parish bulletin, Facebook page, Instagram account, or myParish App for upcoming parish events.

"May God bless you and keep you; the Lord make his face shine on you and be gracious to you; the Lord turn his face toward you and give you peace" (Num 6:24-26).

Fr. James Kaniparampil CMI
